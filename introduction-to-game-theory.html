6 Introduction to Game Theory

Game theory

  • Game theory is the analysis of strategic interactions.

  • A game is a decision problem in which the final outcome depends upon the decisions of all players, as well as the state of the world.

  • A strategy is a complete plan of action that describes what a player will do under all possible circumstances.

  • The payoff matrix represents the payoffs associated with each possible combination of strategies.

    • Describe how every combination of action that each player could take results in payoffs for each player
    • Typically, we think about payoffs as utility , not money. Think about what that means about risk preferences. However in experiments, we pay in cash, not utility.

Best response

A player’s best response correspondence describes the set of strategies that maximize their utility, conditional on their opponent’s strategy.

  • Correspondence rather than function because sometimes there is more than one thing that can maximize your utility

Examples

  • Pure coordination

  • Matching pennies

  • Rock, paper, scissors (assume draw yields zero payoff)

  • Rock, paper, scissors. Players get twice the payoff if they win by playing rock.

Dominant and dominated strategies

A dominant strategy is a strategy that is a best response no matter what your opponent’s strategy is

  • Strictly dominant strategy: a strategy that you would strictly prefer to play for any possible strategy your opponent could play

  • Weakly dominant strategy: a strategy that does at least as well as any other strategy you could play, against any possible strategy your opponent could play

A Strictly dominated strategy is a strategy that does strictly worse against all possible opposing strategy than some other strategy that you could play

Elimination of strictly dominated strategies

One solution concept for games is iterated elimination of strictly dominated strategies:

  1. Rule out all strictly dominated strategies

  2. Restrict analysis to the game where players’ strictly dominated strategies are eliminated

  3. Go back to step 1

Nash Equilibrium

  • Nash equilibrium is a strategy profile such that each player is playing their best strategy given the decision of the other players.

  • Nash’s Theorem states that every finite game that is, every game in which all players have a finite number of pure strategies has a Nash equilibrium.
